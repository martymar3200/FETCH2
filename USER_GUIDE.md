# FETCH2 User Guide

Welcome to the FETCH2 User Guide. This document provides step-by-step instructions for operating the system, broken down by workflow.

---

## 1. Accession Workflow

The Accession workflow is used to group library items into logical entities (Trays or Non-Tray batches) before they move to verification or storage. 

### Starting a Job
1. Click the **Accession Jobs** link in the main navigation menu to open the Accession Dashboard.
2. Click the **Start Accession** button in the top right corner of the dashboard. This opens the "Create Accession Job" modal.
3. In the modal, select either the **Trayed Accession** or **Non-Tray Accession** tab.
    - **Trayed Accession** requires selecting an **Owner**, a **Container Size**, and a **Media Type** from the dropdown menus.
    - **Non-Tray Accession** only requires selecting an **Owner**.
4. Click the **Submit** button. The system generates the job and routes you to the Job Scanning screen.

### Scenario A: Trayed Accession Scanning
1. On the Job Scanning screen, locate the **Scan Tray Barcode** input area.
2. Type the tray's barcode into the field and press **Enter**. The system will register this as the *Active Tray* and display its ID.
3. A new input field labeled **Scan Item Barcode** will appear. 
4. Type an item barcode into the field and press **Enter**. The system will immediately add the barcode to the "Items in Tray" list displayed below the input.
5. **Secondary Actions during scanning:**
    - **Remove Item**: Click the **Undo** (circular arrow) icon button next to an item in the "Items in Tray" list to remove it from the tray.
    - **Edit Tray Barcode**: Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon button next to the "Active Tray" header to manually change the tray's ID via a popup modal.
    - **View Historical Trays**: Click the **Trays in Job** accordion menu at the bottom of the screen. This expands a list of all trays processed in this job. Clicking on a tray in the list re-loads it as the Active Tray.
6. When you are finished scanning items into the current tray, click the **Next Tray** button (or press **T** on your keyboard). This saves the tray, clears the screen, and returns you to the "Scan Tray Barcode" input field.
7. Repeat steps 1-6 for any additional trays.

### Scenario B: Non-Trayed Accession Scanning
1. On the Job Scanning screen, locate the **Scan Non-Tray Item Barcode** input area.
2. Type the item barcode into the field and press **Enter**. The system will register the item and display it as the *Active Item*.
3. **Secondary Actions during scanning:**
    - **Edit Item Details**: Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon button next to the "Active Item" header. This opens the "Edit Item Settings" modal where you can select a specific **Container Size** or **Media Type** for that individual item, overriding the job defaults. Click **Save Changes** to apply.
    - **View Historical Items**: Click the **Items in Job** accordion menu at the bottom of the screen. This expands a list of all items scanned in this job thus far. Clicking an item in the list re-loads it as the Active Item.
4. Continue typing subsequent item barcodes into the input field and pressing **Enter**.

### Job Management & Completion
1. **Pausing or Canceling:** At any point during a Trayed or Non-Trayed job, you can click the **Three-Dot Menu** (More Options) button next to the Job ID in the upper left header. Use this menu to click **Pause Job** (temporarily locks inputs) or **Cancel Job** (aborts the entire job and deletes unsaved scanned records).
2. **Completing:** When you have scanned all barcodes for the job, click the **Complete Job** button in the top right corner.
3. A confirmation modal will appear. Select your desired completion route:
    - Click **Complete** to securely finalize the job and return to the Accession Dashboard.
    - Click **Complete & Print** to finalize the job and immediately trigger a print dialog for the batch summary sheet.

## 2. Verification Workflow

The Verification workflow is used as a secondary quality assurance step. Users rescan containers and items to ensure what was recorded during Accession matches what is physically present before it is transferred into a high-density storage shelf.

*Note: Verification jobs are automatically generated by the system when an Accession job is completed. They cannot be created manually.*

### Finding and Opening a Job
1. Click the **Verification Jobs** link in the main navigation menu to open the Verification Dashboard.
2. Review the data table to locate your job.
    - To narrow the list, click **Show Filters** in the top right.
    - Use the input fields that appear above the table columns to search by **Job Number**, filter by **Job Type** (Trayed vs Non-Trayed), filter by **Status**, or filter by **Assigned User**.
3. Click anywhere on the table row of the desired job. The system will route you to the Job Scanning screen.

### Assigning a User (Supervisors)
If you have the appropriate permissions, you can assign a verification job to a specific user.
1. On the Job Scanning screen, click the **Three-Dot Menu** next to the Job ID and select **Assign User**.
2. An inline "Assigned User" card will appear at the top of the screen.
3. Select the user from the dropdown menu.
4. Click the **Save Edits** button.

### Scenario A: Trayed Verification Scanning
1. On the Job Scanning screen, locate the **Scan Tray Barcode** input area.
2. Type the tray's barcode into the field and press **Enter**. The system will register this as the *Active Tray*, displaying its ID and populating the "Items in Tray" list with all items expected to be inside.
3. A new input field labeled **Scan Item Barcode** will appear. 
4. Type an item barcode into the field and press **Enter**. 
5. As you scan, the system will verify the item. In the "Items in Tray" list, unverified items appear in grey text with a 'Delete' icon. Once successfully scanned and verified, the text turns black and a green checkmark icon replaces the delete icon.
6. **Secondary Actions during scanning:**
    - **Remove Item**: Click the **Delete** (trash can) icon button next to an unverified item. A confirmation modal will appear. Select **Delete Item** to confirm removing it from the tray.
    - **Add Missing Item**: If you scan an item barcode that was not originally accessioned to this tray, a popup modal will appear warning you. Click **Add New Item** to add it to the active tray.
    - **View Historical Trays**: Expand the **Trays in Job** accordion menu at the bottom of the screen. Trays with a green checkmark icon next to them have been 100% verified.
7. When all items in the "Items in Tray" list display a green checkmark, the tray is complete. Click the **Next Tray** button (or press **T** on your keyboard).
8. Repeat steps 1-7 for any additional trays.

### Scenario B: Non-Trayed Verification Scanning
1. On the Job Scanning screen, locate the **Scan Non-Tray Item Barcode** input area.
2. Type the item barcode into the field and press **Enter**. The system will verify the item and display it as the *Active Item*.
3. **Secondary Actions during scanning:**
    - **Edit Item Details**: Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon button next to the "Active Item" header. In the modal, you can update the **Container Size** or **Media Type** if needed. Click **Save Changes**.
    - **View Verification Status**: Expand the **Items in Job** accordion menu at the bottom of the screen. Scanned items will display a green "Verified" badge, while unscanned items display an orange "Pending" badge.
    - **Add Missing Item**: If you scan an item barcode that is not part of this job, a confirmation modal will appear. Click **Add Item** to incorporate it into the job.
4. Continue typing subsequent item barcodes into the input field and pressing **Enter** until all expected items are verified.

### Job Management & Completion
1. **Pausing or Canceling:** Use the **Three-Dot Menu** next to the Job ID to click **Pause Job** (temporarily locks inputs). *Note: Canceling a verification job deletes the job but leaves the source items untouched, unlike Accession cancellations.*
2. **Completing:** Once every tray is verified (Scenario A) or every pending item is verified (Scenario B), the **Complete Job** button in the top right corner will turn green and become clickable. Click it.
3. A confirmation modal will appear.
    - Click **Complete** to finalize the job and advance the items to Shelving.
    - Click **Complete & Print** to finalize the job and immediately trigger a print dialog for the batch summary sheet.

## 3. Shelving Workflows

The Shelving process offers two distinct workflows depending on your facility's operational preference: **Direct To Shelf** (rapid, shelf-first scanning) or **Shelve by List** (curating a queue of containers, with optional system-calculated placement).

### Scenario A: Direct To Shelf
This workflow allows an operator to walk up to an empty shelf, scan the shelf, and scan containers directly into it.
1. Click the **Shelving Jobs** link in the main navigation menu to open the Shelving Dashboard.
2. Click the **Create Shelving Job** dropdown button, then click **Direct To Shelf**. 
    - *Note: If you have a default building mapped to your profile, the job will create instantly. Otherwise, a modal will prompt you to select a Building and click Submit.*
3. On the Job Scanning screen, click the **Start Job** button.
4. Locate the **Current Shelf** input field. Scan the shelf barcode. The system will display the shelf ID as your active shelf.
5. A new **Scan Container Barcode** field will appear, and the **Position #** field will automatically populate with the shelf's next available sequential position.
6. Scan a container barcode. If correct, click the **Shelve** button. The container will move to the "Containers Shelved" list below.
7. Continue scanning containers until the shelf is full. To move to a new physical shelf, click the **Change Shelf** (refresh) icon to clear the current active shelf and scan a new one.
8. When you have finished shelving all your containers, click the **Complete Job** button in the top right corner.

### Scenario B: Shelve by List
This workflow allows an operator to aggregate multiple completed Verification Jobs into a single shelving cart task list.

#### 1. Creating the List
1. On the Shelving Dashboard, click the **Create Shelving Job** dropdown button, then click **Shelve by List**.
2. In the "Job Configuration" step, select a **Building** and a **Shelving Mode** (**Manual** or **PreAssigned**).
    - **PreAssigned Mode** computes the optimal shelf location for each container automatically. You may select constraint checkboxes below it (e.g., "Allow unassigned owner shelves").
3. In the "Add Containers" step, select the **From Verification Jobs** tab. Check the boxes next to completed verification jobs, then click **Add from Jobs**.
    - *Optional: Use the **Manual Scan** tab to type external barcodes.*
4. Click the **Create Job** (or **Create & Pre-Assign**) button located at the bottom right. 
5. If using PreAssigned mode, a "Pre-Assignment Results" dialog will appear summarizing how many items successfully received locations. Click **Start Shelving** to proceed.

#### 2. Executing the List
1. The Job Scanning screen behaves differently based on your chosen Shelving Mode:
    - **If Manual Mode:** Scan the desired **Shelf Barcode** first, input a Position number, then scan the **Container Barcode** to link them.
    - **If PreAssigned Mode:** Scan a **Container Barcode** first. The system will display its "Proposed Location". Go to that location, and scan the **Shelf Barcode to Confirm**. If you need to place the container elsewhere, click the **Use Different Location** button to manually force an override.
2. The "Containers" list below tracks your progress. You can filter the display by All, Pending, Assigned, or Shelved.
3. Once every container in the list achieves 'Shelved' status, the **Complete Job** button will become clickable. Click it to finalize the job.

### Scenario C: Shelving Moves
The Shelving Dashboard also provides tools for relocating items and containers that have already been accessioned or shelved anywhere in the system.

1. On the Shelving Dashboard, click the **Create Shelving Job** dropdown button.
2. Select one of the two Move options:
    - **Move Tray Item:** Used to move individual items from their current location into a different physical Tray.
    - **Move Tray / Non-Tray:** Used to move entire Trays or Non-Tray Items onto a different physical Shelf.
3. On the Job Scanning screen, click the **Start Job** button.
4. The system will first prompt you for the destination:
    - For item moves, locate the **Destination Tray** input and scan the target tray's barcode.
    - For container moves, locate the **Destination Shelf** input and scan the target shelf's barcode.
5. The interface will then prompt you to scan the barcodes of the targets you wish to move.
    - *Note: If you are moving containers to a shelf, the **Position #** field will automatically populate.*
6. Scan a barcode and click the **Move** button. The item/container will appear in the "Containers to Move" list below with a grey 'Pending' status.
7. Continue scanning and adding as many items/containers as needed.
8. When your list is fully built, click the **Complete Job** button. The system will process the batch and commit the permanent location transfers.

## 4. Request Workflows

The Request system manages inbound retrieval orders for items stored within the facility. Requests can be generated manually, imported in bulk, or synced automatically from external Integrated Library Systems (ILS). Once created, requests are grouped into Pick Lists for retrieval.

### Scenario A: Creating Requests
1. Navigate to the **Requests** link in the main navigation menu.
2. On the dashboard, click the **Create** dropdown button to reveal creation options:
    - **Create Manual Requests:** Opens a form where you can enter a target Item Barcode, External Request ID (e.g., your ticketing system's ID), Requestor Name, Priority, Request Type, and Delivery Location. Click **Submit** to finish or **Next** to immediately log another.
    - **Import Requests from File:** Opens a bulk import modal. You can download a blank CSV template, fill it locally, and upload it via the **File Upload** zone.
    - **Sync ILS Requests:** Opens a dialog to manually trigger a synchronization job, pulling down pending requests from associated external tracking integrators.
3. Newly created or imported requests will appear in the table data with a 'Created' status.

### Scenario B: Batching Requests into Pick Lists
To pull items off the shelves, pending Requests must be batched together into a single **Pick List Job** for an operator.
1. From the Requests Dashboard, ensure the view toggle is set to **Request View**.
2. Optional: Click **Show Filters** to filter down available requests by Building, Status, Location, etc.
3. Check the selection boxes on the left side of the table rows for the requests you wish to fulfill. 
4. Click the **Create** dropdown menu and select:
    - **Create a Pick List:** Transforms the selected requests into a brand new job. You will be prompted to filter the requests by a select Building first.
    - **Add to Pick List:** Opens a modal allowing you to append the selected requests to an *existing, active* Pick List job.
5. *Shortcut:* After checking boxes, green Quick Action buttons will dynamically appear at the top-right (**Create Pick List** and **Add To Pick List**) to bypass the dropdown menu entirely.
6. Once successfully assigned, the selected rows will be marked with a 'PickList' status and be ready for retrieval.

## 5. Pick List Workflows

Pick Lists are batched retrieval jobs utilized by operators to locate and withdraw requested items from the physical shelves. Once an operator completes a Pick List, the items are moved into an 'Out' state and are ready for delivery, shipping, or patron pickup.

### Scenario A: Executing a Pick List
1. Navigate to the **Pick Lists** link in the main navigation menu to view the dashboard.
2. The Dashboard displays a list of queued jobs. Note the "# of Requests" and the target "Building" for each job to find the appropriate cart.
3. Click anywhere on a Job row to open its Execution screen.
4. If the job is in a 'Created' state, click the **Start Job** action button to begin.
    - *Note: If a supervisor needs to explicitly allocate the job, they can click the three-dot menu, select **Assign User**, and select an operator before starting.*
5. A 'Scan Item Barcode to Retrieve' input field will become available.
6. Scroll down to the "Items to Pick" table to view the target locations for your items. Walk to the specified aisle or shelf.
7. Pull the requested item and scan its barcode into the system.
    - Upon a successful scan, the item's status in the list will immediately change to a green 'Retrieved' badge.
    - The overall progress bar at the top of the screen will advance.
8. *Handling Missing Items:* If you cannot locate an item on the shelf, you can remove it from your current job by clicking the red **Undo** (Revert Item to Queue) icon in its action column. This ejects the item from your Pick List, returning it to the general pending Requests queue for investigation.
9. Continue retrieving and scanning items until the progress bar reaches 100%. The **Complete Job** button will then become clickable.
10. Click **Complete Job**. A confirmation dialog will appear, allowing you to optionally **Complete & Print** a Batch Summary Sheet to attach to your cart before finalizing.

## 6. Shipping Workflows

Once Pick Lists are fulfilled, the retrieved items are transferred to designated Shipping Bins grouped by their delivery destination. 

### Scenario A: Packing Shipping Bins
1. Navigate to the **Shipping** link in the main navigation menu.
2. Click the **Create Shipping Job** button. The system will instantly generate a new job and redirect you to the Execution screen.
3. The interface requires establishing a relationship between your target **Bin** and the target **Items**. You may execute this in two ways:
    - **Bin-First Mode (Standard):** 
        1. Scan an empty **Bin Barcode**. The interface will lock this bin as your current active container. 
        2. Scan an **Item Barcode**. The system will assign the item to the locked bin. *(Note: If the item's location contradicts the bin's designated delivery location, the system will raise a mismatch error).*
        3. Continue scanning items into the locked bin. To switch bins, click the **Unlock/Change** button and scan a new bin.
    - **Item-First Mode (Discovery):** 
        1. Without locking a bin, scan an **Item Barcode**. 
        2. The system will perform a lookup and tell you exactly which active bin you should use for that delivery location, or prompt you to scan a new bin if one isn't open yet.
        3. Scanning the correct bin will automatically place the item inside it.
4. When all items are successfully packed, click the **Complete Job** action button. The bins will be digitally sealed.
5. *Optional:* You can view and print the Shipping Manifest for a completed job by selecting **View Manifest** from a job's three-dot menu on the dashboard.

### Scenario B: Clearing Bins
When a courier or patron retrieves the bin's contents, the physical bin is left empty and must be digitally cleared so it can be reused for future shipping jobs.
1. On the Shipping Dashboard, click the **Clear Bin** button located near the top-right.
2. A modal will appear. Scan or type the barcode of the emptied bin.
3. The system will confirm the bin is cleared and available for reuse.

## 7. Refile Workflows

When items are returned to the facility from external delivery locations or patrons, they sit in a logical 'Refile Queue'. The Refile workflow groups these items into execution jobs so operators can return them to their permanent shelves.

### Scenario A: Batching Items into Refile Jobs
1. Navigate to the **Refile** section in the main navigation menu.
2. Use the view toggle widget at the top of the screen to switch to the **Refile Queue**, which displays all items waiting to be shelved.
3. Check the selection boxes on the left side of the table rows for the items you wish to batch.
4. Click the **Create** dropdown menu and select:
    - **Create Refile Job:** Assigns the selected items to a brand new refile batch.
    - **Add Item to Refile Job:** Appends the items to an *existing, active* Refile Job.
5. *Shortcut:* Like Pick Lists, you can bypass the dropdown menu exclusively by clicking the green Quick Action buttons that appear dynamically.

### Scenario B: Executing a Refile Job
1. On the Refile dashboard, ensure the view is toggled to **Refile Jobs** and click on your target Job ID row.
2. Click the **Start Job** action button to begin.
3. Take an item from your physical refile cart. Locate the "Scan Item Barcode" input field and scan the item's barcode.
4. A **Verify Refile Location** modal will instantly appear on screen, displaying the precise target location (e.g., specific aisle, shelf, and optional tray) the item belongs to.
5. Walk to the target location. To prove the item is being placed correctly, you must scan the physical **Tray** or **Shelf** barcode requested on the prompt.
6. If the scanned location barcode strictly matches the expected location, the modal will close, the item's status will change from 'Pending' to 'Refiled' (green badge), and the progress bar will advance. Attempting to scan the wrong destination will throw an error to rigidly prevent misplacement. 
7. Continue scanning items, walking to the shelf, and verifying the location until all items have been correctly restored.
8. Click the **Complete Job** button to finalize the restorations.

## 8. Withdrawal Workflows

Withdrawals are used to permanently remove items from the facility. Unlike typical requests which handle temporary patron circulations, items processed through a Withdrawal Job are permanently detached from the warehouse tracking logic. 

### Scenario A: Creating a Withdrawal Job
1. Navigate to the **Withdrawals** link in the main navigation menu.
2. Click the **Create Withdraw Job** button on the dashboard to instantly generate a new job and open its execution view.
3. Build your target list of items to withdraw. You can add items via two paths:
    - **Single Entry:** Scan or type a target Item or Tray barcode in the input field located above the "Items in Job" table.
    - **Bulk Upload:** Click the **Bulk Upload** button to optionally upload a prepared CSV spreadsheet array of barcodes.
4. As items are successfully loaded, they will populate in the job tables displaying their current system status.

### Scenario B: Executing the Withdrawal
The completion of a Withdrawal logically depends on whether you have physically retrieved the items from the shelf yet.

**If the items are still on the shelf (Status = In):**
1. You must retrieve the items before withdrawing them. From your active Withdrawal Job, click the **Create Pick List Job** action button (or **Add to Pick List Job** if appending).
2. The items will be batched to the Pick List queue. An operator must independently execute that Pick List (see Section 5) to physically retrieve them. 
3. Once the Pick List is completed, the items' statuses inside your Withdrawal Job will automatically flip to 'Out'.

**If the items have been retrieved (Status = Out):**
1. Once all items in the batch are in an 'Out' status (meaning they are physically off the shelves), the green **Withdraw Items** action button will unlock and become enabled.
2. Click the **Withdraw Items** button.
3. A confirmation modal will appear. You can choose to simply **Withdraw Items** or **Withdraw & Print** to generate a final summary sheet for your records.
4. The defined items are officially and permanently removed from the system, and their badge status updates to a final 'Withdrawn' state.

## 9. Search & Record Management

The global Search system allows you to instantly locate any entity (Items, Trays, Shelves, or any past Workflow Jobs) and view its comprehensive history and current state.

### Scenario A: Using the Global Search Bar
1. Locate the persistent **Search Bar** in the top application header.
2. The dropdown selector on the left side of the bar defaults to "Item". Click this to change your entity search type (e.g., Tray, Shelf, Request, Picklist, Withdrawal, Accession, etc.).
3. Type or scan the exact Barcode or Job ID Number into the input field and press **Enter**.
4. The system will perform a rigid **Exact Match** search:
   - For **Items**, a quick-view modal overlay will appear on screen displaying its status and physical location. You can click 'View Full Details' from this overlay to open its permanent record.
   - For all **other entities**, you will be directly navigated to their full detail dashboard (e.g., a Job Execution page or a Tray record page).

### Scenario B: Advanced Search & Reporting
If you do not know the exact barcode or need to filter data to build a targeted report:
1. Click the **Advanced Search** button located next to the search bar (represented as a slider-icon button on mobile).
2. A modal will appear displaying detailed filter fields specifically tailored to your selected search type (e.g., date ranges, status filters, owner drop-downs, size classes).
3. Fill out the desired filters and click search.
4. You will be routed to an **Advanced Search Results** table displaying all matching records. 
5. *Generating Reports:* If you searched specifically for 'Items', a **Download Report** button will appear above the results table. Clicking this will instantly generate and download a CSV file of all items matching your parameters, which is ideal for audits or statistical reporting.

### Scenario C: Viewing Entity Records (Record Management)
When viewing the detail page for any physical asset (Item, Tray, or Shelf), the Record Management dashboard provides a highly detailed, chronological snapshot of its lifecycle inside the facility:
1. **General Data:** Displays structural data like media type, size class, designated owner, its assigned container hierarchy (e.g., Item -> Tray Barcode -> Shelf Barcode), and its physical building location.
2. **Live ILS Metadata:** For Items, explicitly click the **Look Up Details** button to execute a real-time integration call to your ILS, pulling live Title, Author, Call Number, and Material Type data directly into the dashboard.
3. **Chronological History:** Visualizes the complete movement lifecycle of the asset. You can click the hyperlinked job ID numbers to trace precisely which workflows the item has passed through (e.g., exactly when an item was Accessioned, Verified, Shelved, Requested, Refiled, or Withdrawn, and by whom).
4. **Audit Trail:** Clicking the **View Full History** button opens a granular, system-level audit log displaying every individual state change trigger and its precise timestamp.

## 10. Administration: Location Management

The Location Management module provides administrative control over the digital mapping of the physical facility. The system utilizes a strict cascading hierarchy: **Building -> Module -> Aisle -> Side -> Ladder -> Shelf**.

### Scenario A: Navigating the Location Hierarchy
1. Navigate to the **Admin** link from the main navigation menu and click into the **Location Manager**.
2. To explore your facility's physical map, use the **Location Navigator** widget on the left side of the screen.
3. You must select locations sequentially from the top down. For example, select a Building first to unlock the Module dropdown. Select a Module to unlock the Aisle dropdown, and so on.
4. As you make selections, the main Data Table on the right will dynamically update to display the immediate "children" of your current filtering level (e.g., selecting a specific Aisle will instantly display its respective Ladders and Shelves).

### Scenario B: Adding and Editing Locations
Administrators can modify the structure or define newly built storage units directly from the grid:
1. Drill down using the Location Navigator to the level *above* where you intend to add a unit. 
2. Click the localized **Add [Location]** action button located at the top right of the table (e.g., "Add Module").
3. A modal will prompt you to enter the unit's logical parameters (e.g., Sequence Number, Sort Priority, Physical Dimensions).
4. To *edit* or *delete* an existing location, click the three-dot action menu on the target row inside the grid. *(Note: Deleting a location is strictly blocked by the system if child items or trays are still digitally registered as residing there).*
5. **Special Function: Insert & Shift Shelves**: When viewing the granular 'Shelves' level, an **Insert & Shift** option becomes available. This action allows an administrator to digitally wedge a newly constructed shelf into the middle of a ladder hierarchy without having to manually recalculate and edit every subsequent shelf's sort priority. 

### Scenario C: Bulk Uploading Locations
When configuring newly built sections of the warehouse, defining ladders and shelves individually through the UI is inefficient.
1. On the main **Admin Dashboard**, locate the **Bulk Upload Locations** action widget.
2. The modal will provide a downloadable `.csv` template specifically formatted for ingesting large arrays of Ladders and their child Shelves.
3. Download the exact template, define your new shelving structural layouts offline according to the layout headers, and verify your configurations.
4. Upload the completed CSV to instantly build out the entire structural block into the database.
